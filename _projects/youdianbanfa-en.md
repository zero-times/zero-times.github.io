## Background

Youdian Banfa addresses a class of situations that are common but difficult to structure. A user may already be dealing with a complaint, a report, exposed personal information or a pile of evidence, yet still be unsure what to do first or how to organize screenshots, links, dates and the desired outcome.

The product is deliberately not positioned as a service that judges a case or guarantees a result. Its fixed boundary is information organization and privacy reminders only: no legal advice, reporting on a user's behalf, rights-enforcement service or promised outcome.

## Product loop

The project runs through one path inside the WeChat ecosystem:

```text
Recommendation traffic / WeChat search
        ↓
Youdian Banfa problem-oriented article
        ↓
The matching Mini Program entry
        ↓
Material organization / redaction / checklist / evidence timeline
        ↓
Return through the WeChat taskbar or Official Account menu
```

The Official Account breaks a confusing issue into steps, checklists and decision order. The Youdian Banfa Assistant Mini Program then supports the next concrete action. An article routes to the relevant feature instead of sending every user to a generic home page.

## How the four tools divide the work

- **Material organization** structures what happened, available evidence, actions already taken and the desired outcome, with generation, copy and local drafts.
- **Pre-publish redaction** helps cover private details in screenshots or chat records and saves the processed result on the user side.
- **Pre-report checklist** checks commonly missed material for a selected situation and allows the result to be copied.
- **Evidence timeline** adds and edits events, then produces an ordered timeline that can be copied when more material is needed.

Keeping the tools separate gives each visit one clear job instead of presenting a single oversized form for every situation.

## Local-first data boundary

V1 centers on local processing and local storage. Drafts, timelines and image-processing results persist on the user side, without login, a complex account model or cloud material synchronization. This is an intentional privacy boundary for sensitive material, not simply a missing feature.

Operational review uses aggregated entry and completion signals: content source, entry type, destination page, and actions such as generate, copy, save and checklist completion. The purpose is to learn which content actually brings a user to the right tool and whether the core task is completed.

## My role

I own the product positioning, the Official Account content system, Mini Program feature design, article-to-page mapping and operating-data reviews. The work continuously connects three questions:

- What problem brings the user in?
- Which action should follow the article?
- Was that action actually started and completed?

## Verified results

A reviewed sample of 17 articles accumulated 13,861 reads, 443 shares and 58 saves, with the top article reaching 5,757 reads. The same data showed that explicit search terms, decision-oriented titles and action order attract stronger demand than broad, loosely related topics.

Mini Program source data for June 2026 provided a second signal: 74.19% of new users came from Official Account articles and 24.19% from the Official Account menu. Articles handle problem discovery, while the menu provides a stable return path.

## Next step

The next phase prioritizes better large-text support, continuation of unfinished work, clearer feedback after primary actions, and a more complete view from article entry to core-task completion rather than expanding the feature count.
